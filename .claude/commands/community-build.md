## The 3 Types of Communities

1. **Product Community (Focus: Support & Feedback)**
   - Users helping users, bug reports, feature requests
   - Examples: Figma Community, Notion Ambassadors

2. **Practice Community (Focus: Skill & Profession)**
   - Gathering people of a specific profession to get better at their jobs
   - Examples: IndieHackers (founders), Dribbble (designers)

3. **Play Community (Focus: Shared Interests/Hobbies)**
   - Gathering around a non-work passion or lifestyle

*Decide which ONE you are building before choosing a platform.*

## The "Cold Start" Playbook (0 to 100 members)

You cannot just "launch" a community. You must seed it manually.

1. **The 10 True Believers**: Hand-pick 10 people you know. Tell them you are building a space and need their help to seed conversations.
2. **The Content Trap**: Don't just invite people. Create a high-value piece of content (report, tool, guide) and make "joining the community" the way to discuss it.
3. **Manufactured Activity**: In week 1, you should be asking 80% of the questions and replying to 100% of the answers. Do things that don't scale.
4. **Welcome Ritual**: Personally welcome (or DM) every single person who joins in the first 3 months.

## Engagement Tactics (100 to 1,000+ members)

- **AMAs (Ask Me Anything)**: Bring in an expert once a month.
- **Show and Tell Fridays**: Dedicated thread for members to self-promote.
- **Weekly Challenges**: Post a prompt related to your niche.
- **The "Introductions" Channel**: Mandatory first step for new members.
- **Community Mastermind**: Small group matching (pair 4 people together for a month).

## Automation & Moderation Stack

- **Zapier/Make**: Automatically invite new Stripe customers to Slack/Discord
- **CommonRoom/Orbit**: Community CRM to identify your top contributors
- **Luma**: For organizing community events / webinars
- **Guidelines**: MUST have clear rules pinned (No self-promo outside specific channels, code of conduct)

## Metrics that Matter

Don't track total members (Vanity metric). Track:
- **Active Members (WAU/MAU)**: % of people who post/react
- **Time to First Response**: How fast a user gets help (vital for product communities)
- **Member-to-Member interactions**: When they talk to each other, not just you (The ultimate goal)
