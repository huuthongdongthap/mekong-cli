## The 4 Types of MVPs

Don't build software to validate if people want software. Use these instead:

### 1. The Landing Page MVP
- **What it is**: A smoke test. A website describing the product with a "Buy" or "Sign Up" button.
- **Goal**: Validate demand before writing a line of code.
- **Examples**: Buffer, Dropbox (video MVP).

### 2. The Concierge MVP
- **What it is**: You provide the service manually to a few highly-engaged customers.
- **Goal**: Validate the solution and learn exactly what the software needs to do.
- **Examples**: Wealthfront (founders manually managed portfolios initially).

### 3. The Wizard of Oz MVP
- **What it is**: Looks like software to the user, but humans are doing the work behind the scenes.
- **Goal**: Validate UI/UX and willingness to pay.
- **Examples**: Zappos (founder took photos in shoe stores, bought them when ordered).

### 4. The Piecemeal MVP (No-Code)
- **What it is**: Stitching together existing tools (Zapier, Typeform, Airtable) to deliver the value.
- **Goal**: Validate the end-to-end flow cheaply.
- **Examples**: Groupon (started as a WordPress blog).

## The 48-Hour Validation Sprint

| Day | Action | Goal |
|-----|--------|------|
| **Day 1 AM** | Define the Hypothesis | "If we offer X, Y audience will pay $Z" |
| **Day 1 PM** | Build Landing Page | Clear value prop + Waitlist/Pre-order CTA |
| **Day 2 AM** | Drive Traffic | Post on Reddit, X, reach out to 50 ICPs directly |
| **Day 2 PM** | Measure & Decide | Did we hit the validation threshold? |

## Validation Thresholds (Go / No-Go)

Set these *before* you launch to avoid confirmation bias.

| Metric | Weak (Pivot) | OK (Iterate) | Strong (Build!) |
|--------|--------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Landing Page Conv.** | < 5% | 5-15% | > 15% |
| **Willingness to Pay** | "I'll use it if free"| Pre-orders with discount | Pays full price over phone |
| **Cold Email Reply** | < 2% | 2-10% | > 10% |
| **User Interviews** | "Nice to have" | "I currently use a workaround" | "Take my money now" |

## "Skin in the Game" Hierarchy

Not all validation is equal. Rank feedback by commitment:

1. **Cash** (Strongest) — Pre-orders, LOIs, actual subscriptions
2. **Time** — Willing to do a 30-min interview or beta test
3. **Reputation** — Willing to share with their network/boss
4. **Data** — Giving their primary email address
5. **Words** (Weakest) — "Great idea, I'd totally use that" (Ignore this)
