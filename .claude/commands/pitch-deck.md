## The 12-Slide Framework

### Slide 1: Title
```
[Company Name]
[One-line tagline that explains what you do]
[Funding round] — [Date]
[Contact: email / website]
```

### Slide 2: Problem (1-2 slides)
```
- The BIG problem you're solving
- Who suffers from this problem?
- How much does this problem cost?
- Show data: market size of the problem
- Make it VISCERAL — investors should feel the pain
```

### Slide 3: Solution
```
- Your product in ONE sentence
- How it solves the problem
- Key differentiator (why yours vs alternatives)
- Screenshot or product demo
- "Before vs After" comparison
```

### Slide 4: Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM)
```
TAM (Total Addressable Market): $XXB
  └── SAM (Serviceable): $XXB
       └── SOM (Obtainable in 3yr): $XXM

Source your numbers! (Gartner, Statista, bottom-up calc)
```

### Slide 5: Business Model
```
- How you make money
- Pricing tiers
- Unit economics:
  - CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
  - LTV (Lifetime Value)  
  - LTV/CAC ratio (aim for ≥3x)
  - Payback period
```

### Slide 6: Traction & Metrics
```
Hockey stick chart showing growth:
- MRR / ARR
- User count
- Growth rate (MoM%)
- Retention rate
- NPS score
```

### Slide 7: Product / Demo
```
- 3-4 key screenshots
- Or embedded demo video (60-90s)
- Focus on UX, not features
- Show the "aha moment"
```

### Slide 8: Go-to-Market Strategy
```
- Customer acquisition channels
- Sales motion (self-serve / sales-led / PLG)
- Current cost per acquisition
- Growth levers for next 12 months
```

### Slide 9: Competition
```
Use 2x2 matrix (NOT feature comparison table):

    High [metric A]
         │
    You ●│
         │    ○ Competitor A
    ─────┼──────────────
         │ ○ Competitor B
         │
    Low  │         High [metric B]
```

### Slide 10: Team
```
- Founder photos + one-line bios
- Relevant experience (why THIS team for THIS problem)
- Key hires planned
- Advisors (if notable)
```

### Slide 11: Financials
```
3-year projection:
         Year 1    Year 2    Year 3
Revenue  $XXK      $X.XM     $XXM
Costs    $XXK      $X.XM     $XXM
Burn     $XXK/mo   $XXK/mo   Break-even
```

### Slide 12: The Ask
```
- Raising: $X at $XM [cap/valuation]
- Use of funds breakdown:
  - 40% Engineering (hire X engineers)
  - 30% Sales & Marketing
  - 20% Operations
  - 10% Buffer
- Milestones this funding enables:
  - Milestone 1 by Q2
  - Milestone 2 by Q4
```

## Pitch Deck Dos & Don'ts

```
✅ DO:
- Max 12 slides, 20 min presentation
- One key message per slide
- Use data, not adjectives
- Tell a story (narrative arc)
- Include your contact info

❌ DON'T:
- No walls of text
- No feature laundry lists
- No "we have no competitors"
- No unrealistic projections without basis
- No tiny fonts
```
