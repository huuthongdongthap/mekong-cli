## Part 1: Market Segmentation & Territory Strategy

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)

| Segment | Definition | TAM Size | Serviceable Market |
|---------|------------|----------|-------------------|

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

#### Primary ICP: Digital Agencies (50-200 employees)

| Attribute | Criteria | Why |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| **Company Size** | 50-200 employees | Large enough to have dev bottlenecks, small enough to move fast |
| **Revenue** | $5M-$50M ARR | Can afford $149-$499/mo, ROI is meaningful |
| **Business Model** | Hourly or project billing | Directly impacted by billing inefficiency |
| **Tech Stack** | Modern (React, Node, cloud) | Ready for AI automation |
| **Pain Point** | Turning down projects due to capacity | RaaS solves this directly |

#### Secondary ICP: SaaS Startups (Series A-B)

| Attribute | Criteria | Why |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| **Funding** | Series A or B ($10M-$50M raised) | Budget for tools, pressure to scale |
| **Engineering Team** | 10-50 developers | Backlog exceeds capacity |
| **Growth Stage** | Scaling rapidly | Need velocity, can't hire fast enough |
| **CTO Profile** | Technical, open to AI | Will approve AI agent adoption |

#### Tertiary ICP: Enterprise Innovation Teams

| Attribute | Criteria | Why |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| **Company** | Fortune 1000, traditional industries | Large budgets, legacy tech debt |
| **Initiative** | Digital transformation | Mandate to modernize, automate |
| **Compliance** | HIPAA, SOC2, industry-specific | Need Enterprise features |
| **Decision Maker** | VP Engineering or CIO | Can approve $499/mo+ contracts |

## Part 2: Sales Motion Design

### Three-Track Sales Engine

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    Revenue Engine                           │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                             │
│  Track 1: Product-Led Growth (Self-Serve)                  │
│  └─→ Pricing page → Free trial → Self-onboard → Expand    │
│     CAC: $0 (organic) | Conversion: 3-5% trial→paid        │
│                                                             │
│  Track 2: Inside Sales (SMB/Mid-Market)                    │
│  └─→ Inbound lead → Demo → Pro tier → Account management  │
│     CAC: $500-2K | Conversion: 15-25% demo→closed          │
│                                                             │
│  Track 3: Field Sales (Enterprise)                         │
│  └─→ Outbound → POC → Security review → Enterprise deal   │
│     CAC: $10K-50K | Conversion: 5-10% POC→closed           │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Track 1: Product-Led Growth (Self-Serve)

**Goal:** Minimize friction, maximize trial→paid conversion.

**Funnel Metrics:**

| Stage | Target Conversion | Optimization Lever |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Landing page → Signup | 5-8% | Hero copy, social proof, CTA clarity |
| Signup → Trial activation | 40-50% | Onboarding wizard, first mission success |
| Trial activation → Paid | 15-20% | Usage milestones, ROI dashboard visibility |
| Paid → Expansion (Pro→Ent) | 10-15% | Usage caps, feature gates |

**Key Plays:**
1. **14-day free trial** — No credit card, full Pro access
2. **In-app ROI calculator** — Show value earned during trial
3. **Automated email sequences** — Day 1, 3, 7, 10, 13 nudges
4. **Usage-triggered outreach** — High usage = sales touch

---

### Track 2: Inside Sales (SMB/Mid-Market)

**Goal:** Convert inbound demos, close $2K-$20K ACV deals.

**Sales Process:**

```
Day 0: Inbound demo request → Auto-schedule (Calendly)
Day 1: Discovery call (30 min) → Qualify, demo, trial offer
Day 1-14: Trial period → Weekly check-ins, usage monitoring
Day 15: Close call → Trial review, ROI proof, contract send
Day 30: Onboarding complete → First value realized
```

**Qualification Framework (BANT + AI Readiness):**

| Criteria | Questions | Green Flags | Red Flags |
|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Budget** | "What's your current dev spend?" | $10K+/mo dev budget | "We need this free" |
| **Authority** | "Who decides on tooling?" | Decision maker on call | "Need to run by committee" |
| **Need** | "What's your biggest bottleneck?" | Clear pain (backlog, hiring) | "Just curious" |
| **Timeline** | "When do you need this live?" | Within 30 days | "Sometime next year" |
| **AI Readiness** | "How do you currently use AI?" | Already using LLMs | "AI is risky/scary" |

**Compensation Plan (Inside Sales Rep):**

| Component | Amount | Conditions |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Base Salary | $80K-120K | Based on experience |
| Commission | 10% of first-year ACV | Uncapped |
| Accelerator | 20% after 100% quota | Kicks in at $500K ARR |
| Equity | 0.05-0.1% | 4-year vest, 1-year cliff |
| **OTE at 100%** | $160K-240K | $80K base + $80-160K comm |

## Part 3: Channel & Partner Strategy

### Partner Tiers

| Tier | Requirements | Benefits | Revenue Share |
|------|--------------|----------|---------------|
| **Registered** | Sign up, complete training | Partner portal, deal registration | 10% recurring |
| **Silver** | 5+ customers, $50K QRR | Dedicated AM, co-marketing | 15% recurring |
| **Gold** | 20+ customers, $200K QRR | Technical certification, MDF | 20% recurring |
| **Platinum** | 50+ customers, $500K QRR | Joint GTM, executive sponsor | 25% recurring |

*QRR = Quarterly Recurring Revenue generated by partner*

### Partner Types

#### 1. Agency Partners (Resellers)

**Profile:** Digital agencies who bundle RaaS into client offerings.

**Value Prop:**
- Bundle RaaS as "AI-powered development" service
- Charge clients $5K-20K/mo, pay $149-499/mo
- Margin: 90%+

**Enablement:**
- White-label dashboard (Enterprise tier)
- Client reporting templates
- Co-branded sales collateral

---

#### 2. Technology Partners (Integrations)

**Profile:** Complementary SaaS platforms (Vercel, Supabase, Polar.sh).

**Value Prop:**
- Joint customers get better outcomes
- Shared distribution (app marketplaces)
- Co-marketing campaigns

**Examples:**
- **Vercel:** "Deploy to Vercel" button in RaaS missions
- **Supabase:** Native database integration
- **Polar.sh:** Payment + subscription management

---

#### 3. System Integrators (Implementation)

**Profile:** Consulting firms who implement RaaS for enterprise clients.

**Value Prop:**
- New revenue stream (implementation fees)
- Differentiation vs traditional consultancies
- Certified training program

**Enablement:**
- Technical certification program
- Implementation playbook
- Lead sharing (enterprise deals)

## Part 4: Pricing & Packaging Strategy

### Current Pricing (Validated)

| Tier | Price | Credits | Target Segment | Margin |
|------|-------|---------|----------------|--------|
| **Starter** | $49/mo | 200 | Solo developers | ~85% |
| **Pro** | $149/mo | 1,000 | Agencies, startups | ~90% |
| **Enterprise** | $499/mo+ | Unlimited | Enterprise | ~95% |

### Pricing Psychology

**Anchoring Strategy:**
```
Starter: $49/mo   ← Anchor (looks cheap)
Pro: $149/mo      ← Target (best value, 62x ROI)
Enterprise: $499/mo ← Premium anchor (makes Pro look reasonable)
```

**Why This Works:**
- Starter exists to make Pro look like a steal
- 5x credits for 3x price = obvious upgrade path
- Enterprise exists for price-insensitive buyers

## Part 7: Competitive Displacement

### Displacing Traditional Agencies

**Talking Points:**
> "Your current agency bills $150-300/hour. RaaS automates the same work for $149/month.
>
> We're not replacing your agency — we're making them more efficient.
> They keep strategy, RaaS handles execution."

**Displacement Proof:**
```
Traditional Agency (20 hrs/mo):
- 20 hrs × $200/hr = $4,000/mo
- Unlimited revisions = scope creep
- Manual work = slow turnaround

RaaS Pro:
- $149/mo flat
- Automated 50-75 hrs of work
- ROI dashboard = provable value

Savings: $3,851/mo = $46,212/year
```

---

### Displacing Zapier/Make

**Talking Points:**
> "Zapier connects apps. RaaS automates your development team.
>
> If your workflow involves writing code, testing, or deploying — RaaS is the answer.
> Zapier can't write code. RaaS can."

**Comparison:**
```
Zapier Professional ($50/mo):
- 2,000 tasks/month
- If-this-then-that logic
- No code writing

RaaS Pro ($149/mo):
- 1,000 credits (50-75 hours equivalent)
- Full dev team automation
- Code, test, deploy autonomously

True cost: Zapier + developer ($3,050/mo) vs RaaS ($149/mo)
```

## Part 10: Risk Management

### Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| **Market Education** | High | High | Double down on content marketing, case studies |
| **Competitive Response** | Medium | Medium | Focus on moat (quality gates, ROI tracking) |
| **Economic Downturn** | Medium | High | Emphasize cost savings vs hiring |
| **AI Safety Concerns** | Medium | Medium | Enterprise security certifications |
| **Talent Retention** | Low | High | Competitive comp, equity upside |

### Early Warning Signals

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Trial activation rate <40% | Onboarding broken | Audit UX, add hand-holding |
| Trial→paid <10% | Value not proven | Add ROI dashboard, extend trial |
| Sales cycle >60 days (SMB) | Pricing/authority mismatch | Re-qualify earlier, add self-serve |
| Churn >8% monthly | Product-market fit issue | Exit interviews, feature gap analysis |
