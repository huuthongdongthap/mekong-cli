## Executive Summary

Sophia AI Video Factory targets video production agencies and content studios seeking to automate workflows with AI. Research identifies 3 primary personas with distinct pain points and willingness to pay.

## 1. Target Customer Personas (ICP)

### Persona A: Boutique Video Agency Owner

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Company Size** | 5-20 employees |
| **Revenue** | $500K - $3M/year |
| **Location** | Tier 1 cities (HCMC, Hanoi, Singapore) |
| **Current Stack** | Adobe Premiere, After Effects, Freelancers |
| **Video Output** | 20-50 videos/month |

## 3. Current Solutions & Why They Fail

### Solution 1: Freelance Editors
- **Pros**: Flexible, low fixed cost
- **Cons**: Unreliable, quality variance, communication overhead
- **Breakage Point**: >50 videos/month = management nightmare

### Solution 2: In-House Team
- **Pros**: Control, consistency
- **Cons**: High fixed cost, training time, benefits
- **Breakage Point**: Demand spikes = understaffed or overstaffed

### Solution 3: DIY Tools (Canva, CapCut, Descript)
- **Pros**: Cheap, easy to start
- **Cons**: Fragmented workflow, amateur output, no automation
- **Breakage Point**: Brand requirements, volume demands

### Solution 4: Full-Service Agencies
- **Pros**: Done-for-you, professional
- **Cons**: $5K-20K/month retainers, slow turnaround
- **Breakage Point**: SMBs can't afford, enterprises want control

## 4. Decision-Making Criteria

### Buying Committee Structure

| Persona | Committee Size | Decision Process |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| Boutique Agency | 1-2 people | Founder decides in 1-3 days |
| Content Studio | 3-5 people | Ops + Finance + IT, 2-4 weeks |
| Marketing Agency | 2-3 people | Producer recommends, CFO approves, 1-2 weeks |

### Evaluation Criteria (Weighted)

| Criterion | Weight | What They Ask |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| ROI / Cost Savings | 35% | "How fast does this pay for itself?" |
| Quality Output | 25% | "Can I see samples in my industry?" |
| Ease of Use | 20% | "How long to train my team?" |
| Integration | 15% | "Does it work with our existing tools?" |
| Support | 5% | "What if something breaks?" |

### Trigger Events (When They Buy)

1. **Growth Pain**: Landed big client, can't deliver with current capacity
2. **Cost Crisis**: Freelance/agency costs became unsustainable
3. **Competitive Pressure**: Competitor launching video-first strategy
4. **Leadership Change**: New CMO/Producer wants to modernize

## Appendix: Interview Script (The Mom Test Compliant)

```markdown
### Warm-up (2 min)
"Thanks for taking the time. I'm researching video production workflows.
There are no right or wrong answers — I just want to learn from your experience."

### Context (5 min)
1. "Walk me through how your team produces videos today"
2. "What's the hardest part about meeting client video demands?"
3. "Why is that hard?"

### Problem Deep-Dive (10 min)
4. "Tell me about the last time you had to rush a video project"
5. "What did you do to meet the deadline?"
6. "What didn't you like about that solution?"
7. "How much time/money does video production cost you monthly?"
8. "How often do you hit capacity limits?"

### Solution Exploration (5 min)
9. "If you could wave a magic wand, what would ideal video production look like?"
10. "What tools have you tried that didn't work out?"

### Commitment (3 min)
11. "Would you be open to trying a new solution if it solved [specific pain]?"
12. "Who else should I talk to about this?"
13. "Can I follow up with you in a few weeks?"
```
