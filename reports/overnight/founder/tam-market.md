## Market Definition

Mekong CLI occupies a newly emerging category: **AI-operated business platform delivered via CLI**. It sits at the intersection of three existing markets:

1. Developer Tools (AI coding assistants)
2. Business Software / SaaS (CRM, ERP, project management)
3. AI Agents / Automation (RPA, workflow automation)

The most accurate framing: **"AI Business Operating System for founders and developers"**

---

## TAM — Total Addressable Market

### Segment 1: AI Developer Tools

Global developer tools market (2025): **$28.5B**
AI coding assistant sub-segment (2025): **$6.2B**
CAGR: 28% → projected $21B by 2029

Key data points:
- 28 million professional developers globally (Stack Overflow 2025)
- 45% actively using AI coding tools (GitHub Copilot survey 2025)
- Average spend on dev tools: $200–$500/year/developer

**Segment TAM:** $5.6B (28M devs × 40% AI-tool adopters × $500 ARPU)

### Segment 2: SMB Business Software

Global SMB software market (2025): **$180B**
AI-assisted business tools sub-segment: **$12B** (growing 35% YoY)
Target: solopreneurs + early-stage founders (0–10 employees)

Key data points:
- 400M+ small businesses globally
- 10M+ "solopreneurs" actively using AI tools
- $49–$149/mo ARPU is well within SMB software spend norms ($300–$600/year typical)

**Segment TAM:** $6B (10M solopreneurs × 60% AI-tool adopters × $100 avg ARPU)

### Segment 3: AI Automation / RPA

Global RPA market (2025): **$13.5B**
AI-native automation (replacing RPA): **$4B** and accelerating
Enterprise buyers: $500–$5,000/mo per team

**Segment TAM:** $4B (conservative, enterprise automation slice)

### Combined TAM

## SAM — Serviceable Addressable Market

Mekong CLI's realistic reach given:
- CLI/terminal delivery (limits non-technical users without web UI)
- English + Vietnamese language (limits non-EN/VN markets for now)
- Open source with paid tier (attracts price-sensitive developers)
- Python install requirement (limits enterprise IT-managed environments)

### SAM Filters Applied

| Filter | Reduction |
|--------|-----------|
| CLI-comfortable users (vs. GUI-only) | 20% of TAM |
| English + Vietnamese markets | 35% of filtered |
| Developer-adjacent founders | 40% overlap |
| Willingness to pay ($49+/mo) | 30% of CLI users |

**SAM Calculation:**
- Developer segment: 28M devs × 20% CLI power users × 35% EN/VN × 30% WTP = **588,000 potential users**
- Founder segment: 10M solopreneurs × 15% tech-savvy CLI users × 35% EN/VN = **525,000 potential users**
- Total SAM users: ~1.1M
- ARPU at blended $80/mo: **$88M/year SAM**

**SAM: ~$88M ARR**

## Market Timing Analysis

**Why now (2026):**

1. **LLM costs collapsed** — DeepSeek R1 at $0.14/M tokens makes per-MCU economics viable
2. **Developer AI adoption crossed 50%** — AI tools are now default, not experimental
3. **Agentic wave starting** — Claude Code, Gemini CLI, GitHub Copilot Workspace all launching in 2025–2026; market education is happening for free
4. **No full-stack business CLI exists** — category gap is wide open
5. **Solopreneur economy growing** — post-COVID remote work normalized 1-person companies

**Risk: market too early?**
- Counter-evidence: `mekong cook` is already useful today without any AI-business framing
- The engineering layer (289 commands) works as a standalone coding tool
- Business layer is additive, not required for initial adoption
