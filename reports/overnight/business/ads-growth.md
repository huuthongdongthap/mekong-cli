# Paid Growth & Ads — Mekong CLI

## Overview
Mekong CLI's primary growth engine is PLG (product-led, content-driven, organic). Paid ads are a post-revenue amplification layer activated once organic channels prove conversion rates and CAC baselines. This report covers paid channel strategy, performance reporting, and growth experiment frameworks.

---

## Paid Growth Philosophy

**Rule:** Do not spend on ads until:
1. Organic CAC is measured and proven (<$50 for Starter)
2. MRR > $10K (enough float for ad experiments)
3. Conversion funnel is optimized (activation rate >65%)

**Reason:** Developer tools with poor onboarding waste every ad dollar sending people to a confusing product. Fix product first, then pour fuel.

## Channel Strategy by Tier

### Phase 1 (Pre-$10K MRR): Zero Paid, All Organic
- Content SEO via `mekong writer-blog` + `mekong seo`
- Social presence via `mekong writer-social-batch`
- GitHub/HN/Reddit organic distribution
- Affiliate program (performance-only cost, not upfront spend)

### Phase 2 ($10K-50K MRR): Targeted Paid Amplification
Activate in priority order:

**1. Twitter/X Ads — Developer Audience**
- Format: Promoted tweets featuring CLI demo GIFs
- Targeting: Followers of GitHub, Anthropic, OpenAI, Cursor, Warp terminal
- Budget: $500/mo
- Goal: Drive installs, measure: install → activation rate
- Expected CAC: $20-40 for Starter

**2. Reddit Ads — r/Python, r/MachineLearning, r/SideProject**
- Format: Promoted posts (not banners — developers use adblockers)
- Content: Tutorial posts, not pure ads — "How I built X with Mekong CLI"
- Budget: $300/mo
- Expected CAC: $30-50

**3. Google Search — High-Intent Keywords**
- Keywords: "ai cli tool", "automate coding tasks ai", "mekong cli", "replace github copilot"
- Format: Text ads to `/pricing` and `/comparisons/*` landing pages
- Budget: $500/mo (post $20K MRR)
- Expected CAC: $40-80

**4. LinkedIn Ads — Founders and Agency Owners**
- Format: Sponsored content (case studies, ROI posts)
- Targeting: Founders at 1-50 person companies, CTO/VP Engineering, Agency owners
- Budget: $500/mo (post $30K MRR, LinkedIn CAC is high)
- Expected CAC: $100-200 (justified for Pro/Enterprise conversions)

### Phase 3 ($50K+ MRR): Scale Proven Channels
- Double down on Twitter (best developer CAC)
- Influencer/sponsorship: sponsor 2-3 developer YouTube channels/newsletters
- Retargeting: Pixel on docs/pricing, retarget abandoners
- Programmatic: DSP-based retargeting for enterprise prospects

## Growth Channel Attribution

### UTM Taxonomy (enforced in all links)
```
utm_source: github | twitter | reddit | linkedin | google | affiliate | newsletter | hn
utm_medium: organic | paid | referral | email | social
utm_campaign: launch-q2 | developer-tools | founder-ops | agency-white-label
utm_content: [specific creative or post identifier]
```

All UTMs tracked through Cloudflare Analytics (free tier) → ingested by `mekong marketing-performance-report`.

### Attribution Model
- **First-touch**: Credit the channel that first brought the user (GitHub star, HN post)
- **Last-touch**: Credit the channel at conversion (email CTA, paid ad click)
- **Primary reporting**: Last-touch (for channel ROI decisions)
- **Secondary reporting**: First-touch (for top-of-funnel investment decisions)

## Ads Creative Strategy

### Developer Ads (Twitter, Reddit)
- Always lead with terminal/CLI screenshot or GIF (authentic to audience)
- Show the command, show the output — zero fluff
- Numbers: "289 commands", "$49/mo", "3 env vars", "30 seconds"
- CTA: "Try free" (not "Buy now" — developers want to test first)

### Founder Ads (LinkedIn, Google)
- Lead with pain: "$2,000/mo on VAs? Try $49."
- Social proof: "Used by 500+ founders" (once true)
- CTA: "See how it works" → demo video landing page

### Retargeting Ads (all channels)
- Docs visitors: "Still setting up? Here's the 5-minute quickstart"
- Pricing page abandoners: "Starter is $49/mo. Less than a developer hour."
- GitHub stargazers who didn't install: "pip install mekong-cli — takes 60 seconds"
